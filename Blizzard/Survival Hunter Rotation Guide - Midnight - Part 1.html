Survival Hunter Rotation in Midnight Season 1

The rotation for Survival Hunter focuses on a streamlined resource management loop. In simplest terms, you'll use as many as possible Kill Command in any given fight to generate enough Tip of the Spear to buff most, if not all, of your other abilities. The important thing to keep in mind is that you don't want to use Kill Command too aggressively, as that means you'll waste the Focus it generates and waste Tip of the Spear stacks.
Resource Management: By far, the most important core to learn as a Survival Hunter is your resource management. Your Kill Command ability is limited by charges and cooldown, and Kill Command is responsible for generating both your resources: Focus and Tip of the Spear. The best way to improve your play is to use Kill Command as much as possible in any given encounter, without over-capping on Focus or Tip of the Spear stacks. In practice, with the optimal talent build, this means you are using two abilities between each use of Kill Command.
Resource Spending: Tying into the above point, it's very important you're spending your resources in a controlled way, while also maximizing the uses of your Raptor Strike without idling too much. Due to our Apex node, more Raptor Strike means more Raptor Swipe, which means you're missing tons of free damage if you're either spending too much (or too little!) Focus.
Cooldown Management: Two of your strongest abilities, Wildfire Bomb and Boomstick are limited by cooldowns. It's important you're keeping these cooldowns in mind, as if you miss a use this can have a cascading effect further down the line and greatly lower your damage over any given encounter, especially in AoE.

Best Survival Hunter Rotation

As a Survival Hunter, your entire gameplay loop and priority system is fueled by Kill Command and thus the very powerful Tip of the Spear and Strike as One enhancements. Your goal is to make sure as many of your abilities are being buffed by these as possible, while also using Wildfire Bomb and Boomstick as frequently as possible.

Below, we break down the main priority lists for Survival Hunter: Single-Target Rotation, AoE Rotation, and your opener sequence.

  Best Survival Hunter AoE Rotation

Our AoE rotation is very similar to our single-target gameplay loop. However, you'll have much more frequent uses of Wildfire Bomb and Boomstick due to two talents recommended in most builds: Wildfire Shells and Lethal Calibration. This means it's extra important to pay attention to your ability's cooldown to not waste uses. Something else to consider is your position, as both Wildfire Bomb and Boomstick deal damage in cone shapes, so you need to make sure you are standing in a way to hit as many enemies as possible.

You want to throw a Wildfire Bomb to get the cooldown running and use a Kill Command shortly after the bomb hits to start fueling your Tip of the Spear loop.  Sentinel Notes

  • The Sentinel opener is slightly less predictable due to the random nature of Sentinel's Mark procs, However, you can somewhat see them coming because their condition to proc is for you to spend a stack of Tip of the Spear. In other words, they can never proc from a Kill Command, but it can proc from anything else that spends Tip of the Spear.
  • A single Wildfire Bomb can consume multiple Sentinel's Mark. In AoE, try to make sure a target that whichever target is affected by Sentinel's Mark is always the primary unfortunate recipient of your Wildfire Bomb, to further empower its innate primary target damage bonus.

Pre-Combat Check

Before any combat happens, it is important for Survival Hunters to make sure the following are done. This applies to raids, dungeons, delves, or even open-world content.
  • Summon your pet - Make sure to summon a pet with a Pet Specialization suited for the content.
  • Apply Hunter's Mark - Use Hunter's Mark on the boss or strongest mob in a pack, increasing all damage on that target by 3%. If playing with multiple hunters, each should pick a different target.

Survival Hunter Major Cooldowns

Survival has access to only a handful of cooldowns in the talent trees; some of them can only be picked up very deep into the tree, meaning you won't often pair them together.

Takedown

Takedown Is an extremely short-duration damage cooldown which should always be buffed with Tip of the Spear. In addition to its high upfront damage, it buffs you and your pets' damage by 20%. It is worth noting that this 20% buff does not affect any damage sources outside of our class-specific damaging abilities, so no trinkets or non-class procs can be buffed this way.
Some talents affect Takedown in various ways:
  • Flanked turns Takedown into an impressively powerful AoE attack. This doesn't inherently change the way your cooldown works, but it may affect the way you'd want to time your cooldown. For instance, without Flanked, you may want to use Flanked before the adds even spawn in, so you don't have to spend the global cooldown during the add's lifespan.
  • Twin Fangs allows Takedown to generate 3 stacks of Tip of the Spear, meaning less of your extremely short cooldown window is spent pressing Kill Command, allowing you to press the buttons that deal more meaningful damage more often.
    This talent somewhat conflicts with  Pack Leader's Stampede! talent, which already incentivizes you to follow up your Takedown with a Kill Command to trigger the stampede and beast as soon as possible.
However, the talents that fundamentally modify Flanked the most are all part of hero-talent specific interactions.

For  Pack Leader, Takedown, as mentioned before, is simply modified by Stampede!. This causes your next Kill Command to trigger Stampede and any of the next     Howl of the Pack Leader Beasts in the sequence.
This doesn't affect gameplay a whole lot beyond that, but it is worth noting that Stampede aims in a direction based on your position relative to your target, which is useful to know when you're trying to optimise the fairly narrow AoE zone.

For  Sentinel, talents impact Takedown to a much bigger degree, and are modified by Moonlight Chakram, Lunar Calling, and Can't Miss, Won't Miss.
  • Moonlight Chakram directly replaces Takedown, using the same keybind, and is a powerful follow-up attack. Moonlight Chakram by itself can be further modified by a choice node, either Twilight Requiem or Stalk and Strike.
  • Stalk and Strike is the only Moonlight Chakram node that directly impacts the way you use Moonlight Chakram, delaying its use until you can benefit from the 10 seconds of Wildfire Bomb Cooldown Reduction.
  • Lunar Calling noticeably boosts the proc chance of Sentinel's Mark during Takedown, allowing you to chuck more dramatically stronger bombs. Stalk and Strike can be especially helpful for this in the event you continue to proc a bunch, reducing the odds for you to run out of bombs to spend your Stalk and Strike procs with.

Flamefang Pitch

Flamefang Pitch deals a chunk of fire damage at a ground-targeted area, and any mobs caught in the blast take some extra fire damage over the next 8 seconds. Essentially, this is a supercharged (but really not that supercharged) version of Wildfire Bomb. As a bonus, it covers the ground in fire, making it much more difficult to see what is going on in melee.

in its base form, it is incredibly simplistic to use; simply use it during Takedown, as it will naturally line up provided that you spent 2 points into Savagery.

Flamefang Pitch can be modified by two talents: Grenade Juggler and Wildfire Imbuement, each changing the way you want to use Flamefang Pitch slightly.
  • With Grenade Juggler talented, you gain an extra charge of Flamefang Pitch, as well as providing 10 seconds of cooldown reduction to Wildfire Bomb. You should ideally avoid using Flamefang Pitch if you can't actually carry any more Wildfire Bomb charges.
    This talent can indirectly impact the power of Lethal Calibration, utilising the extra Wildfire Bomb charges to further reduce the cooldown of Boomstick, which in turn can have large impacts on burst AoE output, as well as allowing you to achieve more specific Boomstick cooldown timings.
  • Wildfire Imbuement adds a small amount of fire damage to each auto attack for the next 10 seconds. This talent is dramatically stronger when dual-wielding, especially when using daggers.

    Daggers can attack up to 4 times faster than a 2-handed weapon; despite this looking powerful on paper, it is a weak interaction in practice. That said, if dual wielding it adds a decent amount of single-target power to a set of talents that is otherwise incredibly weak for single-target purposes.

    With that said, currently, even with daggers it is currently not worth talenting deep into Flamefang Pitch, or even at all.

    The duration of Wildfire Imbuement does not stack with itself, so ideally the second charge of Flamefang Pitch is held if you're also talented into Grenade Juggler, unless you are prioritizing burst AoE output.

Boomstick

Boomstick Is a very high-damaging frontal cone channel that should always be used with Tip of the Spear. It has a fairly long range that can be utilised to hit enemies that are ordinarily too spread out to properly hit while in melee range. A quick Disengage gets you to an ideal distance for something like this, but it isn't strictly necessary in most cases.
  • Boomstick is functionally a 45-second cooldown for single target purposes, or it can have cooldowns as low as 15 seconds while AoEing thanks to Lethal Calibration, a staple talent pick for AoE builds.
  • Wildfire Shells can be paired with this to create a feedback loop, causing a drastically more fast paced AoE rotation.
Something to consider is that although boomstick deals an incredible amount of damage for a single button, it spends at least 2 globals effectively to channel. This makes its single target potency a lot weaker than it looks at a glance, Although it is still worth using, simply never using it at all only results in a ~2.5% dps loss, even when heavily invested into boomstick talents.

Due to the layout of the talent tree we cannot easily ignore these talents, but it may give you some context to how "unimportant" boomstick actually is for Single Target output, meaning if there is ever an opporunity to hold its use to utilise it for AoE, you should consider it.

Survival Hunter Advanced Tips

In addition to Strike as One optimization, Survival Hunter has two other main points of optimization to truly separate yourself from a novice to more advanced Survival player.

Strike as One

Strike as One, despite being a passive talent, is one of your most important sources of damage. It's very important you're proccing this as much as possible, which means you're playing around your Focus management and Kill Command charges.

Positioning

As a Survival Hunter, literally all of your AoE/Cleave depends on your positioning. Wildfire Bomb and Boomstick deal their damage in a cone (from the target and from you respectively) so you need to be aware of your position and place yourself in an angle to hit as many targets as possible with the abilities conal shape. Raptor Swipe and Stampede! also rely on your positioning as well, so it's a good idea to keep track of where you are in relation to the target(s).

Pet Utility

To make sure you're meeting you and your groups needs at all times, we strongly recommend you keep a Tenacity, Ferocity, and Cunning pet in your active pet slots at all times. This means you can flex into more self-sustain and Bloodlust with Ferocity, more pure damage reduction through Tenacity, or external CC cleansing with Cunning.
